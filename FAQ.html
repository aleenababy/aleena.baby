Frequently asked questions

How to be an astronomer?

    To become a professional astronomer, one have to have a Ph.D. degree in astronomy or related fields. It is important to learn Physics, mathematics. Solving differential equations(I know they are scary!) are vital part of your journey. Learning coding will also help your journey, like Python. There are free and certificate courses available in places like Coursera, Udemy etc. Data visualisation tools like, matplotlib, sci-kit are interesting.

How to apply for Ph.D. in Astronomy

    Astronomy have vast variety of topics and you can choose someting which excites you. Once you have found something exciting, look for research groups who are working on the same topics. Then you can talk to some astronomers and scientist about oppertunites. Most of the time we are very excited to talk to you. You can directly apply for PhD advertisements too.

Where can I find Ph.D. advertisements?

    If you know which university you want to apply then please take a look at the career page of that university. Otherwise, you can look into AAS for advertisements.

Do you have any questions?
